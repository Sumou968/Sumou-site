How ASHER Works

Mixed municipal waste (excluding metal, ceramic, and glass) is placed in a sealed ASHER chamber. The system operates at 400–700 °C to dehydrate and thermally treat waste without open combustion, disintegrating material into ash within 30–60 minutes. Emissions are routed through dual water‑scrubber and carbon filtration stages, releasing only compliant vapor (≈pH 8.5).
Typical outlet temperature is below 48 °C—far cooler than conventional incinerators— keeping surrounding areas comfortable and odor‑controlled.

  • No external fuel for heat treatment
  • Closed‑loop, low‑odor work area
  • Up to 99% suppression of key pollutants
  • US EPA emission compliance
